Scientific & Medical Validity

1. Mathematical Foundations

The babelForge engine leverages Algebraic Topology and Simplicial Complexes to model brain networks. Rather than simple pairwise connections (graph theory), the brain processes information in high-dimensional structures called "cliques" and "cavities." The Kuramoto Model governs phase-locking synchronization across these regions.

Source: Giusti, C., Pastalkova, E., Curto, C., & Itskov, V. (2015). Clique topology reveals intrinsic geometric structure in neural correlations. Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences, 112(44), 13455-13460.

2. Network Neuroscience & Medicine

Pathologies are modeled as topological deviations. Major Depressive Disorder (MDD) is represented by hyper-stability in the Default Mode Network (DMN), preventing fluid state transitions (experientially: rumination). ADHD is modeled as hypo-connectivity in the Frontoparietal Control Network, leading to insufficient "gain" to maintain high-dimensional cliques (experientially: attentional drift).

Source: Bassett, D. S., & Sporns, O. (2017). Network neuroscience. Nature Neuroscience, 20(3), 353-364.

3. Pharmacodynamics (Interference Mechanics)

Pharmacological interventions act as exogenous perturbations. Ketamine (an NMDA antagonist) rapidly disrupts DMN hyper-stability and induces synaptogenesis. The simulation models this as an interference pulse (cyan/gold) modifying edge density and resetting hubness constraints, overcoming the topological "rut" of depression far faster than conventional SSRIs.

Source: Krystal, J. H., et al. (2013). Ketamine: A Paradigm Shift for Depression Research and Treatment. Neuron, 79(6), 1170-1178.
