Polypharmacy Interaction Mathematics

The NeuroStack Engine does not use pre-rendered animations. It dynamically computes non-linear drug-drug interactions (DDIs) using vector mathematics and renders the result via a live WebGL topological simulation.

1. Pharmacological Vectors

Every molecule in the database is mapped to a 4-dimensional vector: [Arousal, Dampening, Chaos, Repair]. For example, a classical Stimulant heavily weights the 'Arousal' axis, while a Benzodiazepine weights the 'Dampening' axis. These baseline effects are linearly scaled based on the precise milligram dose selected relative to the compound's optimal therapeutic window.

2. Non-Linear Synergies (DDIs)

When mixing specific drug classes, the engine applies exponential modifiers:

- Serotonin Toxicity: Mixing SSRIs with Stimulants/Empathogens triggers synergistic arousal and chaos.
- Severe CNS Depression: Stacking Depressants (like alcohol and benzos) with Antipsychotics multiplies the dampening effect exponentially, destroying network variance.
- Precision Interference: SSRIs mathematically blunt the efficacy (Repair vector) of 5-HT2A agonists (classic psychedelics or ZB-01), mimicking real-world receptor downregulation.

3. Topological Translation

The 3D ellipsoid brain translates these net vectors into visual physics in real-time:
- Arousal increases the speed and amplitude of the cortical wave.
- Dampening shrinks the lobes, slows the wave, and renders the network a dull grey.
- Chaos introduces erratic X/Y/Z jittering and red emissive spiking, simulating uncoordinated noise.
- Repair expands the nodes organically and harmonizes them into a glowing golden pulse, representing the stabilization of 11D cliques.
