The Mathematical Model

This simulation is not a pre-rendered animation. It is a live WebGL transposition driven by the mathematical principles of topological neuroscience and Kuramoto dynamics.

1. Topological Cliques (The Lobes)

In algebraic topology, a 'clique' represents an all-to-all connected network of neurons forming high-dimensional geometric structures (simplices). The size of each lobe in this simulation is dynamically calculated based on its Clique Participation Count. Larger nodes participate in structures up to 11 dimensions (11D).

2. PTSD Flash (Topological Collapse)

When traumatic recall is triggered, high-frequency spectral noise disrupts global synchronization. Mathematically, the 11D cliques 'shatter' into low-dimensional fragments (max 3D or 4D). You observe this visually as the nodes physically shrink, scatter, and turn red (indicating uncoordinated, localized hyperarousal).

3. ADHD (Inattentive Topologies)

Modeled as a state of 'hypo-connectivity' in the frontal and control networks. The underlying topological dimensionality is blunted (max ~6D). Simulating the dopaminergic agent SynaptoStim (SS-20) mathematically 'upregulates' these nodes, expanding their scale and restoring cohesive wave dynamics until an overdose threshold induces chaotic jitter.

4. Tourette's (Motor Tic Hyper-Sync)

Characterized by pathological 'hyper-connectivity' within somatomotor loops, causing sudden, rigid synchronization spikes (tics). The D2 antagonist DopaReg-D2 (DR-02) acts as a dampening parameter in the Kuramoto model, softening the erratic tics into a smooth baseline wave. Overdosing leads to extreme network rigidity (simulating Parkinsonian-like adverse effects).

5. Novel vs. Conventional Pharmacology

Novel Therapeutics (Precision): Compounds like ZB-01, SS-20, and DR-02 are designed to target the topological phase-locking mechanisms directly, restoring high-dimensional synchronization without destroying the baseline wave dynamics.

Conventional Medications (Standard of Care): Broad-spectrum agents (e.g., SSRIs, Haloperidol) mathematically act as blunt global dampeners. Instead of repairing cliques, they merely suppress overall network amplitude. In the simulation, you'll observe that conventional meds fail to restore the golden optimal state, instead leading rapidly to extreme rigidity, dulling, or severe toxicity at higher doses.
